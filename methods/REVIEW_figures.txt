====================================================================
IMPORTANT LIMITATION — Fig v is PSEUDOBULK, not single-cell
====================================================================
The user requested "a UMAP of the CD4+ cells". A true single-cell UMAP was determined to
be INFEASIBLE with the available resources and is reported transparently rather than faked:
  - The 12 donor x condition cell files total ~1.74 TB (each ~140 GB, ~3.07M cells).
  - Their X matrices are stored as ~1.7M tiny HDF5 chunks; over the public S3 endpoint
    (~10 MB/s, no random-access index) a single scattered cell-row read costs ~12 s, and
    even reading the 25 MB indptr does not complete in 600 s.
  - Module-KD cells are fully scattered (2,191 per file = 2,191 singleton row-runs across
    the whole file) so there is no contiguous block to stream; per-row extraction would be
    ~60 h/file and whole-array streaming ~4 h/file, x12 files.
  - No remote compute target was available (list_compute empty).
Fig v is therefore built at the PSEUDOBULK level: UMAP of 11,184 module-KD + NTC pseudobulk
profiles (the same objects used for the DESeq2 reproduction), colored by ISG-core score,
faceted silencer-KD vs NTC. This shows the requested biology (the interferon program rising
under silencer knockdown) and is fully reproducible locally. The figure title and this
review both label it "pseudobulk" explicitly. The single-cell version is deferred to an
environment with either the cell-ranger-indexed local h5ads or adequate remote compute.
