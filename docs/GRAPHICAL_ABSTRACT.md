# Graphical abstract

**Figure concept** (schematic — hand-drawn/vector, not a generative image; the quantitative panels it summarizes come from the real analysis scripts in `results/`):

*Left — the locked state.* A resting human CD4⁺ T cell. Endogenous retroviruses ("viral fossils") sit clamped under a **SETDB1/HUSH H3K9me3 lock**, with the adjacent interferon-stimulated genes (ISGs) dark/OFF.

*Center — releasing the lock.* CRISPRi **knockdown of the SETDB1/HUSH machinery** removes the H3K9me3 lock → the **ERV-adjacent ISGs switch ON**, and the de-repressed ERV emits **double-stranded RNA** (the viral-*mimicry* ligand — not infectious virus) → an **interferon (cytokine) burst**. *(Depict interferon as radiating cytokine spheres, not antibodies; depict the released species as a dsRNA hairpin, not a virus particle.)*

*Center as a rheostat — the bidirectional axis.* A dial / double-headed arrow shows the two **opposite polarities of one axis**:
- **Dial UP** — release the lock (knock down the writer/reader) → IFN ↑ → **viral-mimicry immuno-oncology** ("cold" tumor warming, immune-effector infiltration).
- **Dial DOWN** — reinforce the lock (inhibit the eraser) → IFN ↓ → **restrain interferon-driven autoimmunity** (normal tissue protected).

*Inset — druggable epigenetic nodes.* **Writer** (SETDB1/HUSH) · **Eraser** (KDM4) · **Reader** (HP1/CBX).

**Caption:**

> In resting human CD4⁺ T cells, the SETDB1/HUSH H3K9me3 machinery keeps endogenous retroviruses — ancient "viral fossils" co-opted as *cis*-regulatory elements of interferon genes — switched off. Genome-scale CRISPRi knockdown of the silencing module de-represses interferon-stimulated genes enriched next to these silenced ERVs (confounder-controlled ~2× enrichment; SUV39 specificity control; authors' differential expression reproduced at r = 0.94), revealing an ERV-gated interferon program rather than the hypothesized Th1/Th2 axis. This defines a **bidirectional, druggable interferon rheostat**: dialing the axis *up* (releasing the lock) drives an ERV-derived interferon state, while dialing it *down* (reinforcing the lock) restrains it. The two **predicted therapeutic directions** — viral-mimicry immuno-oncology and restraint of interferon-driven autoimmunity — are motivated hypotheses; no therapeutic effect is demonstrated here.

**Two-line figure legend** (for the submission / a slide):

> **The SETDB1/HUSH–ERV–interferon axis is a bidirectional, druggable rheostat in human CD4⁺ T cells.** Releasing the H3K9me3 lock (CRISPRi knockdown) de-represses ERV-adjacent interferon-stimulated genes and triggers an interferon response; the two directions shown — viral-mimicry immuno-oncology (dial up) and restraint of interferon-driven autoimmunity (dial down) — are *predicted* therapeutic hypotheses, not demonstrated outcomes.
