# Written summary (Cerebral Valley submission form)

**Title:** TE-aware target discovery in a T-cell Perturb-seq dataset — the SETDB1/HUSH machinery gates an ERV-encoded interferon regulatory program.

**What we found.** The Marson/Pritchard genome-scale CRISPRi Perturb-seq dataset is read out on a probe-based 10x Flex panel that is structurally blind to transposable elements — the exact layer the H3K9me3 silencing machinery controls. We built a TE-aware analysis of this TE-blind dataset. Knocking down the ERV-silencing module (SETDB1, TRIM28, ATF7IP, HUSH) de-represses genes that are modestly but robustly enriched near silenced (H3K9me3-marked) ERVs (confounder-controlled OR ~1.7–2.2, three methods × three conditions; the SUV39H1/2 methyltransferases, which act off-ERV, do not enrich — a clean specificity control). Functionally, those ERV-proximal de-repressed genes are dominated by an interferon program (IFN-γ over-representation FDR 3×10⁻¹³; IFN-α the top positive GSEA hallmark in all three conditions), including checkpoint-relevant CD274/PD-L1 and known ERV-enhancer-controlled genes (APOL family). Because the induced interferon genes sit *near* the silenced ERVs, the data support ERVs acting as **cis-regulatory elements of interferon genes** (the MER41/AIM2 co-option paradigm), gated by SETDB1/HUSH — an inducible/repressive regulatory axis, distinct from trans "viral mimicry" (which this TE-blind assay cannot measure). Among druggable nodes, the KDM4/JMJD2 "eraser" KDM4C is the lead directly-measured candidate: its knockdown produces an ERV-proximal signature antagonistic to SETDB1, and it has selective sub-100 nM chemistry.

**The honest arc.** We started from a mouse hypothesis (Adoue 2019) that this axis tunes Th1/Th2 balance. We confirmed a modest, confounder-controlled ERV-cis-enrichment with clean specificity — but the Th1/Th2 module scored null. Following the data with an unbiased functional enrichment revealed the ERV-gated interferon program as the real immune consequence, which also explains the null (the knockdown hits the ERV/interferon regulatory layer, not the T-helper lineage machinery).

**Demonstrated vs. hypothesis.** Demonstrated (this dataset): the ERV-proximal interferon de-repression; the modest cis-enrichment with SUV39 specificity; independent reproduction of the authors' DE (r=0.94); KDM4C as a directly-measured druggable node. Hypothesis (not shown): any therapeutic application, and whether the ERVs are functional enhancers (cis-proximity is not proof). The ERV→interferon relationship and the MER41/AIM2 mechanism are established literature; the contribution is the clean, unified demonstration in primary human CD4⁺ T cells.

**How Claude Science contributed.** Autonomous multi-stage pipeline (remote S3 data slicing on a laptop; pseudobulk DESeq2; ERV-cis enrichment; ORA + direction-aware GSEA; ChEMBL tractability), with named reproducible artifacts, Plan-mode checkpoints, and a Reviewer agent that caught a figure-significance error and confounder issues.

**Why it matters / next directions.** The axis is a tunable, bidirectional interferon rheostat with disease potential in two directions — *release the lock* to induce an ERV-driven interferon state in checkpoint-refractory tumors (viral-mimicry immuno-oncology), or *reinforce it* to restrain pathogenic interferon in type-I-interferon-driven autoimmunity. The drug-discovery frontier runs writer → eraser → reader nodes, with the on-mechanism reader layer (HP1/CBX) pointing toward targeted-degrader modalities and structure-guided modality selection. A TE-aware assay (to separate cis regulatory-element from trans viral-mimicry mechanisms) and a perturb-then-checkpoint design are the two experiments that would move this from mechanism toward therapy. Roadmap, not results — but it is why the axis is worth drugging.
